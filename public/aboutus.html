Overview

Since 2020, the Covid-19 pandemic has been devastating and brings unmeasurable pains to global lives. Besides the physical threats, health issues, and financial burdens it caused, mentally stress is also a leading problem because most people need to follow social distancing and physical lockdowns. Among these mental health issues, Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD) is one of the most affected group in this pandemic.
Today, Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD) affects more than 15 million people in the U.S., with women four times more likely to be diagnosed with it than men. Under the condition of lockdowns and social distance, people do not regularly expose to the sunlights as much and rarely communicate with others in person. These issues of lack spontaneity and random events, limited social interaction, and reduced light exposure are leading factors that could put more people under the threats of SAD. Furthermore, many individuals minimize their symptoms because they experience guilt and shame or feel weak conveying that the weather affects their mood (Lindberg, 2020), which could worsen their conditions when they actually need help. Therefore, we definitely see a gap that needs better solutions, and come to our problem statement: How might people in inclement weather areas with SAD achieve a stronger sense of connectedness and better self-care techniques so that they can improve their mood?

Our actions

Our designed solution is a functioning webapp that merges basic chat and recommending features into weather forecast platform to make sufferers feel less alone. It employs certain online “therapies” as a supplementary treatment to the SAD group who wants to alleviate symptoms and seeking privacy at the same time. Through recommendation algorithms, it feeds users with sunnier locations to increase light exposure and cheerful music as a SAD mind-body therapy. It also aims to facilitate connectedness and overall give the SAD group better coping strategies and support.

Target stakeholders and users

This webapp is designed to help people who are under SAD impacts, no matter who’s formally diagnosed or just experiencing related symptoms and stress. Besides them, other people from the general public are also welcomed to use this web for better moods and networks. Users with severe symptoms who need professional and immediate help may not be suitable for this.

Benefit of our solutions

Our solution approach aims to help people understand SAD is validating, make sufferers feel less alone, and facilitate connections. Compared to pervasive weather app on the market, our site is trying to demonstrate forecast information with more empathies and good vibes using pleasing images and neat UI.
At the same time, our key feature pages(travel, music, chat) are all helping the SAD group through multiple ways(facilitating social interactions and networks of self help, meditation & mindfulness, and increasing light exposure & outdoor activities), which are all proved methods that could help alleviate SAD symptoms.
