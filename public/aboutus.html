Overview

Since 2020, the Covid-19 pandemic has been devastating and has brought unmeasurable global pain. Besides the physical symptoms, health issues, and financial burdens it has caused, mental stress is also a large problem because people need to follow social distancing guidelines and live under lockdown restrictions. Those who have Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD) has been one of the hardest hit groups by the pandemic.

Today, Seasonal Affective Disorder affects more than 15 million people in the U.S., with women four times more likely to be diagnosed with it than men. Under the condition of lockdowns and social distancing, people are more likely to get less exposure to sunglight and have reduced hours of social interaction. These issues of a lack of spontaneity and random events, limited social interaction, and reduced sunlight exposure are leading factors that could exacerbate SAD or cause SAD in persons previously unaffected. Furthermore, many individuals minimize their symptoms because they experience guilt and shame surrounding their SAD and admitting that the weather affects their mood (Lindberg, 2020), which could worsen their conditions. Therefore, we see a problem that needs better solutions, and come to our problem statement: How might people in inclement weather areas with SAD achieve a stronger sense of connectedness and better self-care techniques so that they can improve their mood?

Our Actions

Our designed solution is a functioning webapp that merges basic chat and recommendation features into a platform to make sufferers feel less alone. It employs certain online “therapies” as a supplementary treatment to the SAD group who wants to alleviate symptoms while simultaneously seeking privacy. Through recommendation algorithms, it displays to users sunnier locations to increase their light exposure and music playlists as a SAD mind-body therapy. It also aims to facilitate connectedness and overall give the SAD group better coping strategies and support.

Target Stakeholders and Users

This webapp is designed to help people who suffer from SAD, no matter if they are formally diagnosed or just experiencing related symptoms and stress. In addition, people who do not have SAD are also welcome to use this web app to alleviate their mood and foster connectedness. This app may not be suitable for users with severe symptoms who need professional and immediate help.

Benefits of our Solution

Our solution approach aims to help people understand SAD and help sufferers feel validated, help sufferers feel less alone, and boost their mood. Out of all the apps that aim to tackle mental health problems, few are designed specifically for those who suffer from SAD. Furthermore, compared to pervasive weather apps on the market, our site is trying to demonstrate forecast information with more empathy using pleasing images and UI.

At the same time, our key feature pages(travel, music, chat) aim to help the those with SAD through facilitating social interaction, meditation & mindfulness, and increasing light exposure & outdoor activities, which are all proven methods that could help alleviate SAD symptoms.
